# Vision

StudyDesk exists to provide a serious, local-first place to manage the study loop:

```text
Create or import material -> review it -> track progress -> identify weak spots -> repeat
```

## Problem

Students often split their workflow across multiple disconnected tools:

- one app for flashcards
- another for notes
- another for quizzes
- and often a generic chat tool with no durable study context

That fragmentation creates friction, weak long-term tracking, and repeated setup effort across subjects and study modes.

## Product Direction

StudyDesk is intended to remain:

- offline-first
- portable
- structured for active studying rather than generic note capture
- consistent across flashcards, quizzes, and future study formats

## Principles

### Offline-first

Core study flows should remain usable without an internet connection.

### User-owned data

Study content should remain portable and understandable rather than locked into opaque storage or account-dependent workflows.

### Practical cross-platform support

The architecture should support mobile-quality study flows while remaining friendly to desktop and web testing.

### Real utility over placeholder breadth

A smaller number of complete, reliable workflows is more valuable than a wide surface of unfinished features.

### Optional AI

AI may support selected workflows in the future, but it should never be required for StudyDesk to remain useful as a study tool.
